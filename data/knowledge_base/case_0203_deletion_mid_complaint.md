# Resolved case — customer requested account deletion during unrelated complaint

Case ID: CASE-0203

Situation: A customer called about a delivery delay, and mid-call said
"actually just delete my whole account and all my data, I'm done with this
company."

Resolution: Even though the call started as a standard DEL-007 delivery
issue, the data deletion request immediately triggers DPR-001 — it must be
escalated to the data protection team regardless of how it arose or what
the original reason for contact was. The agent should still resolve the
original delivery delay issue if the customer wants that handled first
(in this case, a $10 credit was issued for the delay), but the deletion
request itself goes to a separate DPR-tagged ticket, not the standard
escalation queue.

Note for future agents: a data privacy request embedded inside an unrelated
call still requires full DPR-001 handling. Do not treat it as a throwaway
comment. Confirm to the customer that the deletion request has been logged
separately and will be handled within the 72-hour DPR SLA.
