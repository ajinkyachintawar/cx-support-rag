# Resolved case — customer threatened negative social media post

Case ID: CASE-0171

Situation: A customer, frustrated about a delayed delivery, said during the
call "if this isn't fixed today I'm posting about this on every platform I
have." No mention of legal action or regulatory complaint, just a social
media threat.

Resolution: A social media threat alone does NOT meet the escalation
criteria under ESC-002 — escalation triggers are legal action, regulatory
complaint, or media contact specifically, not social media posts generally.
The agent handled this within standard delivery delay process (DEL-007) —
the delay was 5 days past estimate, so a $10 service credit was offered,
same as any other case at that delay length. The customer's frustration
level does not change the policy applied, only the tone of the response.

Note for future agents: do not over-escalate based on customer frustration
alone. Apply the standard process and de-escalate with empathy in the
agent's own words, but the policy outcome stays the same as a calm customer
with the identical delay.
