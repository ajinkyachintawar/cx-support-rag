# Resolved case — customer disputed charged amount due to currency conversion

Case ID: CASE-0211

Situation: Customer in a non-USD region said they were charged more than
the listed price. Investigation showed the listed price was in USD and the
customer's card was charged the converted local currency amount, which
included their bank's own conversion fee, not ours.

Resolution: This is not a billing error on our end — the listed price was
correct in USD, the discrepancy came from the customer's bank's conversion
rate and fees, which we have no control over and do not reimburse. The
agent explained this clearly and pointed to the order confirmation showing
the original USD amount charged. No refund or credit applies. This is
explicitly NOT a promo code or billing system issue (see PROMO-021), it is
a currency conversion matter entirely outside our system.

Note for future agents: always check the order confirmation's listed
currency before assuming a billing error. If the USD (or listed currency)
amount matches what was supposed to be charged, the discrepancy is a bank
or card network conversion issue, not ours to resolve.
